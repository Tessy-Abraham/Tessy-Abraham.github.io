Ernakulam bordering the district of Thrissur in the north, Idukki in the East, Alapuzha and Kottayam in the south, Lakshadweep Sea in the west is an amalgam of a hoary heritage and global growth of industry and commerce. For the outside world, Ernakulam is part of Kochi referred to in the history of Kerala as the Queen of Arabian Sea. The densely populated Ernakulam District embodies the achievements of the Kerala State in literacy, industry, trade and commerce. With an inclusive culture, relatively high per capita income and an unstoppable interest of the people in updating themselves of the political, economic, social and cultural happenings in the national and international spheres, Ernakulam represents the high modern phase of the Kerala society.

Ernakulam District came into being in 1958 April 1 by incorporating Kanayannoor, Kochi, Kunnathunadu, Aluva and Paroor Taluks. Deliberation for hours held at the Kerala Fine Arts Hall in 1957 and attended by a host of political, social and media activists was instrumental in the formation of the District. The etymology of the term ‘Ernakulam’ is linked to different sources, either temple-oriented or mythological. KomattilAchutha Menon attributes the origin of the word from a type of mud called ‘Erangiyal’. Lord Siva was addressed in Chennai as ‘Erayanar’in the past. In Kerala too, this was followed and it later came to be known as ‘Ernakulam’. Ebanubathootha who travelled along the Kerala coast between AD 1342 and AD 1347 did not even make a casual reference to Kochi. The name ‘Kochi’ is a combination of two terms, Koch&Azhi (small river-mouth).
There’s nothing more ecstatic than exploring the local markets in Kochi. The vibrant colors, oh-what’s-that kind of products, the bustle that never seems to end, what can be more cultural and enriching than a trip to these clusters of hubs where you can just shop it all. Let’s start this curated list with some of the coolest malls you can visit:
Best Places For Shopping In Ernakulam
  • Lulu Mall Kochi : This biggest mall in India (in terms of land area) is undoubtedly the one-stop destination for shopping, dining, and entertainment in Cochin and definitely comes in the top of the list of shopping malls in Kochi.
  • The ABAD Nucleus Mall : With just 66 stores predominantly comprising of Indian brands like Max, FabIndia, Aurelia, Big Bazaar etc., The ABAD Nucleus Mall is relatively small but with an equally chic shopping experience. The Bandhan boutique and Voylla stores offer some innovative clothing and jewelry.
  • Centre Square : Centre Square has most of the commonly known elite brands like Nike, Arrow, Basics Life, Vero Moda, Park Avenue, Reliance Trends etc., making it an ideal choice for quick shopping in Cochin. Brands like Decora, Swiss Time, and Club Burgoyne cater to the visitor’s loves for international brands.
  • Marine Drive : The Marine Drive by the backwaters of Kochi is shopaholic’s delight! The GCD complex and Bay Pride have an assortment of stores that sell imported electronics, perfumes, and clothing. The Flea Market here has a great collection of casual cotton clothing at dirt cheap prices.
  • Jew Town : The Jew Townhouses a number of shops and warehouses that sell antiques. You need to carefully check out the shop to get some good antique pieces at a fraction of the price that might cost elsewhere.
